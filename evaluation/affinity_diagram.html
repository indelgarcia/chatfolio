ChatFolio - Think-Aloud Study Synthesis

Affinity Diagram  ·  7 Groups, 5 Participants Each  ·  6 Themes Identified  ·  April 2026

🚫  Onboarding & Initial Prompt 6 / 7 groups
Initial prompt is too prescriptive - suggests "house" or "retirement" as the only examples, boxing users in.
Users felt like they had to fit into a predefined category even if their goal was more general (e.g. "just grow my money").
Bot should ask about age, income, debt, and life stage before jumping to goal selection.
"Additional Info" section at the end of the flow feels like an afterthought - that context should be gathered upfront.
Better opener: "Tell me about yourself and what you're hoping to do with your money" instead of listing specific goals.
Bot jumped straight to timeline questions before understanding the user's life situation.
Users had to volunteer age, job, and income info themselves - the bot never proactively asked.
When multiple goals were typed, model only addressed the first one.
Example goals (retirement, house) skew older - not relatable for 22-year-olds just starting to invest.
"It should feel like a conversation with a financial advisor who wants the whole picture."
Need: prompts for projected income, rent, existing savings, student debt, and upcoming life changes.
⚠️  Risk Tolerance Confusion 3 / 7 groups
"35% confident" is a confusing and unusual metric - users didn't know how to interpret it.
Risk level descriptions aren't sufficient for beginner investors - they need more context.
Needs a visual spectrum or labeled scale (e.g. "Conservative → Aggressive") explaining each level.
Newcomers fundamentally don't know what "risk" means in an investing context.
Should ask "How experienced are you with stocks or finances?" early in the flow to calibrate language and recommendations.
💬  Chat & Conversation Flow 5 / 7 groups
Users dumped all their inputs into the first message, bypassing the intended step-by-step flow entirely.
Chat persists after portfolio creation - users were unsure if it was for follow-up questions or to restart from scratch.
Follow-up responses after portfolio generation were abrupt and provided insufficient feedback.
When a user asked the chatbot to explain the "additional info" field, it skipped the explanation and jumped straight to portfolio generation.
It's not clear to users that the chatbot can assist them during the profile generation process.
Follow-up questions from the bot used financial jargon that newcomers didn't understand.
No visual indication that sending a follow-up message triggered a portfolio regeneration - output appears with no transition.
"More feedback if you send another chat - it's too abrupt and not clear it produced a new output."
📊  Portfolio Output & Comprehension 5 / 7 groups
ETF tickers (VTI, VXUS, etc.) are confusing for beginner investors - most don't know what they are.
Users don't know what "indexes" mean or where the specific funds come from.
Portfolio percentage breakdowns are confusing - hard to connect to real money or to their specific goal.
Users want a deeper dive into what each index fund does and why it's in their portfolio.
Only Vanguard funds shown - users felt the selection wasn't diverse enough.
Should allow users to set a target dollar amount (e.g. $1M) instead of just a vague physical goal.
Hard to understand how the portfolio connects to a goal as specific as buying a house in a particular city.
ETFs and financial terminology should be defined inline - not hidden in dropdowns or tooltips.
🖥️  UX & Visual Polish 4 / 7 groups
Page auto-scrolls to the bottom during portfolio generation - users have to scroll back up to see the beginning.
Text color when referencing user input values was inconsistent with the rest of the chat UI.
"How to Get Started" section is hidden in a dropdown - it should be visible by default for new users.
No loading indicator when a follow-up message triggers portfolio regeneration - users don't know if anything is happening.
Mandatory vs. optional input fields are not clearly differentiated - users unsure what to fill in "Additional Info."
Post-generation content is unclear: users don't know if additional follow-up questions are changing the portfolio or not.
✅  What Worked Well 6 / 7 groups
Loading screen was helpful - users appreciated knowing the system was processing and not frozen.
Sidebar updating in real-time as users answered questions was widely appreciated.
Chart is clear, optimistic, and intuitive - users could read it without explanation.
Portfolio explanation section was well-received once it loaded - users liked seeing the reasoning.
"What is ChatFolio?" disclaimer was useful - users who read it upfront felt more oriented.
Conversational format overall was liked - users preferred it to a traditional form.
Users appreciated that the model visibly reflected their inputs in its recommendation.
Data presentation is clean and easy to follow - diversification mix made sense once explained.

Top Design Insights

1
Context before goals. The single biggest pain point across all groups: the bot starts with "what's your goal?" before it knows anything about the user. Age, income, debt, and life stage should be gathered first - this foundational context shapes every recommendation that follows.
2
Beginner literacy gap is critical. Multiple users didn't know what ETFs, indexes, or risk tolerance meant. The interface currently speaks to someone with baseline financial knowledge. Inline definitions, tooltips, and a literacy check early in onboarding would close this gap.
3
Follow-up feedback loop is broken. After the portfolio is generated, any follow-up interaction is confusing - users can't tell if their message did anything, what changed, or whether the chat is for editing the portfolio or starting over. The regeneration state needs clear loading feedback and diff-style output.
4
The conversational format is an asset - invest in it. Users consistently liked the chat interface and real-time sidebar. The opportunity is to make it feel more like an advisor and less like a form: ask follow-up questions, be curious, and make users feel understood before recommending anything.
